Common names

Jaddah.

Discription

Small shrub, 20-40 cm long, branched at the base, covered with thick, greyish, or white woolly pubescence. The branches are cylindrical, tapering at both ends, ascending to erect, each terminating in a comb or panicle inflorescence. Leaves simple, sessile, oblong to blunt apex, crested edge, curled sharply downward, seldom flat.

Geographical distribution

Mediterranean, western Iranian - Turan, spread to a limited extent in the reefs in the north of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, also it is spread in the Mediterranean region.

Environment and Reproduction

It grows on the sides of rocky plateaus, and in small streams of sandy loamy soils, often rocky. It prefers sunny locations, neutral and alkaline lands. It multiplies by seeds.
