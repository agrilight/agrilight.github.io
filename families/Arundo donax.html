Common names

Persian reed, Qaisob, Al-Ghab, Al-Qalam.

Description

Perennial herb mediated by creeping rhizomes, bearing tuber-like bulges. Stem hollow, bamboo-like, erect, smooth and bare, base is 2 cm or more thick, can reach 6 m in height.

Environment and Reproduction

It grows heavily in the agricultural areas, watersheds, springs, springs, irrigation channels and agricultural drainage on the Arabian island. It reproduces by seeds and rhizomes.

Pastoral importance

It has little pastoral value, and young plants (50-100 cm high) are more palatable than those developed in the early years.
