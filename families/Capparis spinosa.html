Common names

Shafallah, Spiny Tomb, Malath.

Discription

Stem. Its branches are long (1-2 m), lying down or ascending, smooth and barren, and its color tends to purple or bluish-green. Leaves simple, long-hanging, rounded or ovate, completely edged, barren; The ears are transformed into strong thorns adorned with the head and varying in length.

Geographical distribution

Mediterranean extending to the Iranian-Turanian region, widespread in the Arab world, including Kuwait, Qatar, and the peninsula, Arabian island.

Environment and Reproduction

It grows in semi-arid and dry areas on stony slopes, steppes, roadsides, valley sides, near walls and fences near human habitation, in deserted places and limestone lands, and on the layered and fractured limestone dolomite rocks, and it exists in deep alluvial soils. Light-loving, well-drained soil, tolerates low temperatures. Seeds are light-loving, as their germination rate is greater if they are exposed to light, that is, they are on the surface of the soil, which dries quickly and therefore their germination should be under abundant water conditions allow the seed to germinate and the seedling to grow and stability, and therefore this species grows in places with bountiful water resources. Capers are cultivated in some southern European countries.
