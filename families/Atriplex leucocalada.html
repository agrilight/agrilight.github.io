Common names

white-branched ragul, Syrian ragul

Discription

Perennial woody base plant, 30-100 cm tall. It has a semi-spherical shape, covered with short gray fluff, and gives it a white flour resulting from the expulsion of salt by the plant.

Geographical distribution

This species belongs to the Turan-Iranian and Saharan-Arabian phytogeographic region. It is common in very dry bioclimatic floors with cold winters. It grows naturally in Saudi Arabia, Jordan, Palestine, Kuwait, Iraq, Bahrain, Syria, Egypt and Iran.

Environment and Reproduction

It is found on the edges of valleys, streams and in depressions. The white-branched ragul is one of the plants tolerant of rust and salinity. The fruits ripen in the fall and can be planted by direct scattering.

Pastoral importance

After the white groats, the branches are one of the best pastoral plants in terms of nutritional value and ability to graze due to the abundance of its vegetative growth and containing a high amount of indigestible protein, as its percentage is equivalent to what is found in Hijazi clover. Throughout the year, whether it is bare or bass, it is a declining plant because of its weak resistance to overgrazing. It is characterized by a short growing season and ten months are limited to 4 months April - May - June, and it enters 4 semi-dormant phase after that.
