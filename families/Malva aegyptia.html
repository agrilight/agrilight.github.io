Common names

Egyptian hibiscus.

Description

Annual herb, winter, mober, 10-20 cm tall. The market is numerous, ascending to erect. Leaves 1-4 cm; the lower is long-hanging, and the upper is semi-sessile, the blade of the leaf is rounded or semi-rounded in its general form.

Geographical distribution

The species is endemic to the Mediterranean region, and spreads in the dry and very dry deserts, in Syria, Palestine, Jordan, Lebanon, Iraq, northern Arabia, Libya, Egypt, Tunisia, Algeria, Morocco, and Mauritania.

Environment and Reproduction

It is a beautiful season plant. It adapts to drought by reducing its leaf area and its deep lobes. Lowlands are preferred. It spreads in the steppes, and on the slopes with sandy soils or silt rich in nitrogen. It is propagated by seeds sown directly into the soil at the beginning of spring.

Pastoral importance

The grazing animals are well cared for, especially goats and sheep, with other grazing species. If they are aloes, they harm the young sheep, causing intestinal poisonings that may lead to the death of newborns.
